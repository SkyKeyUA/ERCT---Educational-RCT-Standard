Abstract

In a randomized field trial with 2,850 seventh-grade mathematics students, the authors evaluated whether an educational technology intervention (ASSISTments) increased mathematics learning when used for homework. The intervention provided automated feedback and hints for students, while delivering organized performance data to teachers so they could adapt their instruction. Results showed significant gains in math test scores, especially for students with lower prior achievement.
